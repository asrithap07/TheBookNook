Book Club Starting Resources

Book Club Advertisement

To recruit members for your book club, you can start by gathering close friends, then begin to advertise utilizing flyers and social media posts. If it's a school club, you can also set up information station in an area of the campus or advertise your club on the morning announcements.

Meeting Preparation

Before each meeting, the club manager should be sure to communicate to each member three important pieces of information: the meeting location, what material/which chapters will be covered, and what items to bring.

Meeting Content

Before getting into discussion questions, it might be beneficial to review the previous chapters, questions, or content from the previous meeting. After doing that, you can begin your discussion about the book or chapter that the group has been reading. Some examples of discussion questions are:

1. What was your favorite part of this chapter/book?

2. What was your least favorite part? Why?

3. Do you have any unanswered questions?

4. What are your predictions?

5. What new information did you gather about [character]?

6. What place in this book would you most like to visit?

7. Would you read another book by this author? Why or why not?
